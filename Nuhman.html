I'm Nuhman, and These Are My Favorite Movies

Three films that stayed with me long after the credits rolled.

No. 01

Interstellar (2014)

Christopher Nolan's epic science-fiction masterpiece follows astronaut Cooper and a team of scientists who travel through a wormhole near Saturn in search of a new home for humanity. As Earth's resources dwindle and crop blights threaten civilization, Cooper must make an impossible choice — save the world or return to his children. With breathtaking visuals, a hauntingly beautiful Hans Zimmer score, and a story that ties love and physics together in ways that linger for days, Interstellar is unlike anything else in cinema. It reminds you that the universe is enormous, but the bond between a father and daughter is even larger.

No. 02

The Dark Knight (2008)

More than a superhero film, The Dark Knight is a riveting crime drama that asks hard questions about justice, chaos, and what it costs to be good in a corrupt world. Batman faces his greatest threat yet — the Joker, a criminal mastermind with no motive beyond pure anarchy. Heath Ledger's iconic, Oscar-winning portrayal of the Joker remains one of the greatest screen performances ever captured on film. Nolan crafts every scene with the precision of a thriller and the weight of a tragedy, making this a movie you return to again and again, finding something new each time.

No. 03

Finding Forrester (2000)

William Forrester, the reclusive author of a single celebrated novel, crosses paths with Jamal Wallace — a 16-year-old basketball prodigy from the Bronx with a secret passion for writing. What unfolds is a beautiful mentorship story about talent, trust, and the courage it takes to share your inner voice with the world. Sean Connery delivers a warm, commanding performance as the aging writer who rediscovers his own purpose through a young man who refuses to be boxed in. Finding Forrester is quiet, tender, and deeply human — a film that makes you want to pick up a pen and write the first sentence of something honest.
